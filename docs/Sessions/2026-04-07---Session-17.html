2026-04-07 - Session 17

2026-04-07 - Session 17

5 min read

As the party hides themselves in an alcove in the cliff face just outside Bad Canyon, Tantaka limps into the canyon as a distraction. After a couple taking a hits from hurled rocks, Tatanka rounds the corner again at a run with two large ogres in close pursuit.

Numyr steps out and holds the line in front of the larger ogre. Molly snipes him over Numyr’s head while Arlo charges him on Tatanka. Wayne scrambles up the cliff face behind them to snipe from above.

The younger ogre brother moves in to hit Molly, and Wayne shoves a boulder down the cliff onto him. Then, with a groan and meaty thumb, the older ogre collapses to a quick pommel blow from Numyr. Numyr pops one of Smyrna’s loquacious licorices. His breath suddenly smells like hot tar and creosote, but he is able to communicate with the younger ogre. He tell him to haul his brother away if he doesn’t want to see him killed.

The younger ogre, already surprised to see his mighty brother felled, drags his unconscious brother back by the wrist. With the way forward now clear, the party advances into the canyon.

As they approach the cave on the back wall, the ogre behind them hollers a warning. Molly eats a peanut ear. She suddenly goes deaf, but she finds she can hear people’s thoughts if she focuses intently on them.

When they reach the cave, Numyr peers into the dark and sees a large ogre slipping into cover behind a wall of thorny brush. Numyr advances, shield at the ready, and the ogre charges him, swinging a small tree trunk driven through with old swords and bits of metal.

The hulking ogre then retreats back into the darkness of the cave, but Numyr pursues. When he casts an arcing witchbolt, he draws the ogre out once more, but the ogre lays into three of the party with a great swing of his tree-trunk club. Wayne lassos him. Arlo charges him on Tatanka and peppers him with arrows. Molly shoots him, and then rushes forward to stab him, but she is knocked down by his club. Numyr calls a healing word to her and she crawls back to her feet.

In the distraction of battle, Wayne lights the wall of brush aflame and dashes past the surrounded ogre into the darkness of the cave. He finds a young female ogre chained near a large pile of bedding behind the brush. Wayne tosses her a crowbar to free herself, and then he shoots back at the hulking ogre, who is now surrounded by the rest of the party and silhouetted against the entrance of the cave. Blorugg, cruel Pappy of the ogre clan, falls dead.

As smoke from the burning brush begins to fill the cave, the young ogress pulls her chain spike free of the stone. Wayne and Numyr hurry past a second wall of brush to find Mora—blinded, disfigured, and imprisoned—in a back room of the cave.

Numyr quickly explains why they have come, and he hands her the bloodied egg. Although she pauses for a moment, considering his words, she eats the egg. Her eyes and fingers heal, and her tongue reforms. When she reaches out to lay a hand on Numyr, he feels painfully dizzy for a moment as something passes between them.

Once Mora has freed herself from her own chain and has grabbed a few essential supplies from a stash in the back of the cave, she and her daughter flee with party back into the open air.

At the cave mouth, Mora spits on Blorugg’s corpse and scans the empty canyon. She is relieved to learn that the party has not slain any of her children. (Molly catches her daughter, Sheeka, explaining that the youngest, Grunt, was killed by unknown humans a few days previously.)

Over a shared snack of rations and water, Numyr describes where Old Krorg’s portal cave lies. Mora says she will contact the Troll Witch for more instructions. Mora’s conscious son, Little Grod, hollers again from the prairie beyond the canyon, and she yells for him to come in.

With the remains of the ogre family reunited, the party takes their leave. On the way out, Wayne spots a couple drifting slips of paper.
