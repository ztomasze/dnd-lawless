2026-03-17 - Interlude 1C

2026-03-17 - Interlude 1C

9 min read

The Shaggy Man, played using Don’t Rest Your Head rules, concludes.

Wendy, Benny, Debbie, and Ailbhe stand just outside the Deadbridge mine as dusk falls. They hide the string of stolen thermalights in Debbie’s school bag and throw a sack that Debbie brought over the sol-fire. They haul them to Petey’s house.

Once there, they hide in the bushes out front while Benny gives Ailbhe a boost up to the roof over the doorstep. From there, Ailbhe sneaks into Petey’s room through the window he left ajar. She can hear Petey and his mom cleaning up after dinner, so she hides in Petey’s closet.

Once his mom has tucked him in and left a nightlight candle burning, Ailbhe whispers to Petey from his closet. Petey gives a yelp! After his mother has checked on him again, Ailbhe sneaks downstairs to the front door to let the others in with the heavy gear. She has to slip through the shadows as Petey’s mom tidies up for the night. Ailbha begins to panic as the shadows start to close on in her again, but this time she pushes her way through the fear, actually daring the shadows to come at her. When they do, she pulls them in to disguise and muffle her friends as the group climbs the stairs to Petey’s room.

There, they make some plans and move some furniture, such as Petey’s trunk and hat rack. They then settle down to wait, with Benny under a blanket at the base of the bed and the rest of them under the covers by Petey’s knees. Petey blows out the candle just in time so that his mother does not get a good view of the several lumps under his covers when she checks on him before turning in. Soon the house grows quiet.

Suddenly everyone feels it: There is a presence in the room. Benny hears the creak of the floorboards and a rustle as Petey pulls the blankets up over his head.

Then Benny launches their plan into action. He leaps up from under his blanket and throws the lid open on Petey’s trunk. The glowing thermalights fill the room with light. The Shaggy Man, standing next to the bed, raises one hand to shield his eyes while he brings the other down at Benny. That hand bears a curved black blade. However, Benny is already onto phase two of the plan. The blade thunks into the wooden floor behind him as he sprints forward, the end of the thermalight string in one hand, rounding the assorted bases of laundry they’d laid out on the bedroom floor.

As the string of lights closes around the Shaggy Man, shadows stream from his form. He stumbles back, abandoning his blade, and leaps for the closet door. He passed through the door with a burst of shadow.

Benny, still mad from his sprint, chases after him, but wrenching open the closet door reveals only hanging clothes.

The children regroup. Petey can still feel the Shaggy Man, not far off. The Shaggy Man is a little shaken from their ambush but very angry too. They’ve rattled him, but he’s still a threat. They have his knife now, but the handle is made of insubstantial shadow while the blade is like sharp obsidian.

Not sure what to do, they stow the lights back into the trunk and lie in the dark, whispering possible plans to each other. Eventually they fall silent for a while.

In that silence, they hear light scurrying footfalls and a quiet voice muttering to itself. Benny throws open the trunk again, revealing a foot-high figure with an enormous nose and a small rag, which it is using to polish at the knife gouge in the floor.

Once it realizes the children can see him, the old killmoulis introduces himself as Nedward. Puzzling over how they can see him, he conjectures it is because they are caught between the worlds. As he put is, if the Shadow realm is one slice of bread and the Mortal realm another, then the children are now caught in Evernight, the peanut butter that forms the sandwich between the two. The bogeyman they call the Shaggy Man likely did this to them. However, because he didn’t pay the cost of pulling them into the Evernight peanut butter, they gained the energy of that transfer as special powers. The Shaggy Man will likely continue to haunt the children, bleeding that energy off them of them in some way to his own ends, such as using it to form his own portal so that he can fully enter the Evernight or even haunt the Mortal realm.

The children try to buy old Nedward’s help with promises of treats like porridge, butter, milk, or raisins, but the killmoulis adamantly refuses to get involved in such fey politics. Although he doesn’t have anyone else to recommend, he points out that the children could follow the Shaggy Man through the closet themselves.

They get out of bed, pull Petey after them, disconnect the thermalights, and push the trunk into the closet. When they close the door and feel around the back wall in the pitch black, they find that they can shove their way through the clothes into another closet. When they open the door of that closet, they find themselves in a ruined shadowy version of Petey’s parents’ room. Through gaping holes in the wall, they see a featureless black sky. The air is filled with a faint blue light with no apparent source.

Through the holes in the wall, Wendy hears moaning and a rattle of chains drift up from the ground floor. She slips over and peeks down through the broken window. A grey-skinned white-haired child looks up at her with huge haunted eyes. The child holds up her hands in pleading supplication, revealing that she is missing a forefinger. A chain dangles from a manacle around her wrist. When Wendy steps back from the window in alarm, the child panics and begins to mournfully moan.

At that, the group hears something large get to its feet around the corner, in what would be Petey’s room. They quickly spring into action. Benny starts to hammer the shadow blade into his bat, but, on this side of the closet, it is the blade that is insubstantial instead of the handle. He starts strapping it on to the bat instead.

Wendy runs to the bedroom door and closes it. The door has been slashed open at some point, leaving wide jagged cracks running down its face. Through the cracks, she can see the Shaggy Man step from Petey’s room. Wendy begins to quail, playing that she is alone and lost and defenseless. The Shaggy Man grins and strides forward.

As he does, Debbie and Ailbhe shove the trunk against the closed bedroom door. The Shaggy Man peers in at them through the cracks, growling and befuddled to learn that there are more of them. With a roar, he smashes open the top half of the door and starts to climb over the bottom half.

Debbie sends her hair forward in a burst. It wraps around his neck, pins his arms, grips the door frame, and pushes him back. As the Shaggy Man gags in the doorway, Ailbhe darts forward, throws back the lid of the trunk, and screws in the thermalights. The Shaggy Man reaches a clawed hand out for her, but Ailbhe fades into the shadows.

And then Benny rounds the corner, hefting his blade-strapped baseball bat to his shoulder. With a flick of a trading card from his fingers, the spectral wings of a dragon appears in the shadows on the ceiling over his head. He steps up onto a corner of the trunk; the thermalights stream their light up into the face of the entangled Shaggy Man; and Benny swings as hard as he can for home.

His bat gives a resounding crack as it smashes through the Shaggy’s Man face. The bogeyman’s body drops to the floor.

Quickly, not knowing if this might shift the balance of energies at play or draw other players to this part of the stage, the party disconnects the lights and shuts the trunk. As they slide it back to the closet, Wendy pulls a necklace of fingers from the Shaggy Man’s chest. One of the fingers looks fresh, so she gives it to Petey, who helplessly holds it against his severed knuckle. Wendy tosses the rest of string to the pale child out the window. The child seems too far gone for speech, but her face grows tranquil as she looks down at the fingers she holds in her hands.

The party pulls the closet door closed behind them, and they shove their way back through the hanging clothes. The clothes seem denser this time, but they push harder and soon open Petey’s closet door.

They are home again. Petey lets go of the finger and holds up his hand. It is whole, his finger restored.

After catching their breaths, the children realize that they lost the blade somewhere on the other side of the closet. They bid farewell to Petey and slip out of the house. They leave the sol-fire and the thermalights by the mining company warehouse, where they will be found by Wallop in the morning. Then they hurry home and fall into their own beds, exhausted. Blessedly, sleep comes close behind.

In the morning, the sky has returned to normal. At school, they share broad smiles. Wendy passes Petey a jar filled with porridge, liberally sprinkled with raisins.
