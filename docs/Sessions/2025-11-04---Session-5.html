2025-11-04 - Session 5

2025-11-04 - Session 5

5 min read

The party stands over the body of Old Krorg, and everyone but the owlbear catches their breath. With a rag from Arlo, Numyr wipes Old Krorg’s blood from his face. Beneath the blood, his skin feels oddly cool, somewhere between tingling and numb. The feeling slowly spreads and then subsides.

Arlo leads most of the party in skinning the owlbear, while Numyr leads Ralph through the rest of the cave, looking for other passageways or any precious ores. After Numyr’s comment about the owlbear’s blood, Arlo is not sure whether his own blood-slick hands feel strange or not.

At the back of the cave, Numyr finds a steep climb up to another opening about 18 feet up. He scales the rock face, with his glowing shield on his back. He feels a warm humid draft on this face as he peers into the gloom at the top. He sees three statues of some sort of small wild goblin, along with two glowing pale blue flames. Peering at those flames causes him to feel cold inside. Snapping free of that paralyzing reverie, he scrambles back down to the cave floor and calls for the others.

Wayne tries luring the fire-eyed beast into view with a hunk of owlbear meat on a rope, but it doesn’t bite. He breaks out his climbing kit so that Numyr can scale the center of the rock face and try to lasso one of the statues without looking into the glowing eyes. When he has trouble with the lasso, Wayne takes his place. He successfully pulls the small but heavy chalky statue towards him. However, at the lip of the cave mouth, the statue suddenly halts. Instinctively glancing back at it, Wayne finds himself locking gazes with a squat multi-legged thorny lizard with eyes of blue flame. Wayne freezes, feeling himself growing cold inside… but, when he distantly hears Numyr call his name from below, Wayne wrenches his head aside. He scrambles back to the ground.

Numyr tries pulling the goblin statue down, but the lizard has a firm grip on it. It jerks it back into its lair, severing the rope around it.

After two close encounters with the creature, the party decides to leave this strange beast alone for now. They return to skinning the owlbear, pausing to discuss whether taking a few talon trophies would reduce the bounty value of the hide. They decide to leave it intact.

The hide is very heavy, since many of the feathers along the back are stiff, heavy, and almost chalky. They work together to get it out of the cave and onto one of the horses.

The party returns to Deadbridge in the mid-afternoon, heading straight to Beaumont Beeves. They meet Clarence Beaumont and his secretary Edmund Lyle. Mr. Beaumont pays them the full $100 bounty. Arlo offers to work as a rancher for Beaumont, starting in a couple days, to which Mr. Beaumont replies that he would be amenable.

Arlo evenly deals out the reward money to all involved, saving the leftover $2 for a first round of drinks at Holler’s. On the way there, Numyr stops in at the Sheriff’s to warn her about the fire-eyed lizard, as they had warned Mr. Beaumont. Both of them seem slightly skeptical and fairly unconcerned.

At Holler’s, the party shares their hunting tale with the miners there, many of whom troop out to see the hide for themselves. Drinks and celebrations continue until dinner time.

Bidding farewell to Ernie, Ralph, and Ginty, the party heads back to the hotel for their dinner. Again, Numyr says he’ll catch up with the others. He stops at Curios & Wizardry, where he meets the sage, Maximillian Schrodenberger, whom everyone else seems to call Old Max. The old man identifies the lizard as a basilisk and the wild goblin variant as a gremkin, a kind of savage fey goblin that tends to run in packs. Old Max related the lore that a creature can be unpetrified through a special alchemical solvent made from the basilisk’s bile. The basilisk is also susceptible to its own mirrored gaze, although this can be unreliable and would likely petrify the bile gland along with the rest of the body. Numyr leaves a couple silver coins as thanks for the information. He also fails to impress Max’s aloof black cat, Ophelia, before departing.

After Numyr relates his findings to the rest of the party over his dinner, they all stroll to Mort’s for a final nightcap. They get a chance to see the near-dead miners for themselves, and they learn that the night shift strike is ongoing. Mort agrees that perhaps he should contact a solicitor from back east for advice.

After their long day, the party turns in at the hotel again.

When they wake in the morning, the men of the party find their chamber pot tipped over, knots in their bedsheets, tangles and knots in their hair, an owlbear feather on the floor, and the word “konsikwenses” scratched on their small wash stand mirror. Molly has similarly tangled locks and sheets and a feather of her own. None feel that they are fully rested, as if some vague weight now hangs upon them.

They wonder if Ernie and his crew might have been similarly affected and whether this will change their morning plans to protect Mad Mattie and Gus’s stagecoach run to Caspar.
