2026-03-10 - Interlude 1B

2026-03-10 - Interlude 1B

5 min read

The Shaggy Man, played using Don’t Rest Your Head rules, continues.

Wendy, Benny, Debbie, and Ailbhe stroll from Petey’s house down to Main Street, looking at the thermalights and chained sol-fires. There are none that could be easily swiped, so they follow the string of lights across the bridge and head for the mine.

A miner there blocks them from going in. Aibhe says she’s there to see her father, “Jim Dawson”, and so the miner steps back into the mine to look for him. Aibhe follows him, hoping to either duck into the shadows or casually stand aside if the miner should return. However, the shadows start gathering in around her, and she chokes. She runs back to the light and the rest of her friends waiting for her on the bridge.

The children cross back over the bridge to see if there might be any other sol-fires they’d overlooked. Poking their head into the Mining Company workshop, Wendy starts a conversation with Wallop the gobber. She learns that there aren’t any handy sol-fires around and that the sturdy stone storage shed in the center of the mining company courtyard holds excavated phi-stones awaiting shipment. When Wendy then fights back some tears over her fear of the coming dark, Wallop makes her a small nightlight.

Still hopeful to find keys to the sol-fire padlocks or more information about the mine, they head to the mining office. Again Wendy takes lead, aiming to distract the mining director, Walter Movart. Wendy exclaims that she just saw a little goblin messing with one of the sol-fires, so Movart hauls himself to his feet. Escorting Wendy, he stops by the workshop to collect Wallop and then they head to the Sheriff’s office.

During the hub-bub, Debbie and Benny hang back. Debbie talks Benny into breaking the office window, and then he stands guard while she slips in to look around. She doesn’t find much regarding the sol-fires, but she does see a large map of the mine and the shift schedule, which shows that the mine will be clear this evening. Benny has to run a distraction op with a “lost dog” story as a miner passes by so that Debbie can slip out again unseen.

Meanwhile, Wendy leads Ailbhe, Movart, Wallop, and the sheriff down to the water tank and the sol-fire that powers the pump there. Wendy successfully plies her tale of the little gremkin. Aibhe sadly interjects that gremkin killed her mother. In the awkward silence following, the Sheriff says she will set up a patrol for the night to make sure there aren’t more in the area.

As the townsfolk return to their jobs, the party regroups behind the hotel and discusses their next move. The mine seems like their best option, but it’s nearly dinner time. They separate and head home for now to eat and check in with their parents.

A couple hours later, they meet back at the bridge. They cross over to the mine, which is now locked shut. However, the gap under the entrance grate is just wide enough for them to squeeze under.

Inside, they find that there is a small sol-fire in the first large room of the mine. It is chained to an iron spike driven into the stone. Using a crowbar and Debbie’s help, Benny is able to pry the spike loose. Both are breathing hard from the effort, remembering how long it has been since they slept. They pull the sol-fire out of the niche it is in and disconnect its string of lights. They start pulling the light string down from the line of nails it is sitting on.

As they do, the thermalights further into the mine begin to flicker and dim. Glancing in that direction, they see an enormous spider—bigger than any of them—drifting down the tunnel toward them!

Wendy flips on her her nightlight and starts running back toward the entrance, leading the way. Debbie runs behind her, pulling down the string of thermalights and gathering them in her arms. Aibhe hauls the sol-fire, while Benny turns back to the face the spider.

After drawing a trading card from his pocket, Benny gives a dragon-like roar. The shadow of a long neck and and a jet of flame seems to flicker around him as he lays into the spider with his baseball bat.

The spider stumbles to one side, but it also knocks past Benny to shoot webbing after Aibhe. However, as the webbing floats to the ground, it reveals that Aibhe is no longer there. Benny hauls himself back to his feet, swinging his bat again. The spider checks itself and scurries backwards. Aibhe steps out of a shadowy alcove. As the spider retreats, she and Benny turn and run after the others.

Everyone scrambles under the mine gate. They are just barely able to shove the small sol-fire through with a shriek of metal.

Sunset is now at hand, and so nightfall is not far behind. Clutching their stolen sol-fire and bundle of thermalights, they head for Petey’s.
